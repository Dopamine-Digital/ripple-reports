What this means for you: when classification is handled by operations staff on top of their primary duties, error rates run higher and the work takes longer. Multi-terminal network
Website / caribtrans.com/locations
Caribtrans operates 8 US receiving terminals (Miami HQ, Atlanta, Houston, LA, New Jersey, New York, Orlando, Canada) plus international offices in Panama, Dominican Republic, St. Maarten and St. Lucia. Cargo flows inbound from all of these points into US customs entries.
What this means for you: multi-origin consolidations mean different people at different terminals are describing cargo for the same customs entry. Without a centralized classification system, the same product can get different HTS codes depending on which terminal received it. That inconsistency is invisible until an audit surfaces it.
